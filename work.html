Featured Mandate  ·  Ratings Recovery

A rating floor lifted —
and held.

A large consumer finance application, operating with a review ecosystem exceeding 100,000 ratings, where the aggregate store rating had settled below the threshold at which it materially affects install decisions. At that scale, a fractional shift in rating is not cosmetic — it moves conversion at the point of decision and shapes acquisition economics downstream.

→
Rating, after 4.2
Timeframe 2 months
Review base 100K+

The approach

The work began with a review-signal audit: isolating the recurring complaint patterns driving negative ratings, separating genuine product friction from noise, and mapping where sentiment was concentrated. From there, the response architecture and feedback loop were rebuilt so that resolved issues translated into recovered ratings rather than silence. A quality-led approach — the underlying review dynamic addressed at the source, with the resources and execution depth that large review bases require.

Why it held

Recovery is the easier half. On a base exceeding 100,000 reviews, a two-month gain can erode just as quickly without a system behind it. The rating was stabilised at its new level and maintained — monitoring, response discipline, and ongoing management kept the floor in place rather than allowing it to drift back. The result was durable, not a spike.
